Feature Preprocessing

  • StandardScaler applied to regularize classical models and stabilize DL gradients.
  • ARX Logic: 7-day trailing lag windows engineered to flatten the temporal dimension for MLR, RF, and XGBoost.

Final Generalization Performance

Classical ensembles achieved near-perfect mapping on the Snow Catchment (NSE > 0.96) but failed drastically on the Rain Catchment.

Interpretability: Snow Catchment Success

  • White-Box Extraction: RF Gini importance reveals the model prioritized dayl(s) (Daylight).
  • The Physics Learned: Daylight is the perfect proxy for seasonality and continuous solar radiation.
  • Conclusion: Smooth, highly-autocorrelated target variables are easily captured by decision tree thresholds without overfitting.

Error Analysis: The High-Variance Problem

  • Extreme Bias: Models completely blind to Top 10% peak flow events.
  • Loss Function Penalty: Because models were trained on MSE, missing a massive flash flood spike carries a huge mathematical penalty.
  • The algorithms "learned" to conservatively predict the historical mean to minimize overall loss.

Feature Engineering Investigation

Hypothesis: Are models missing soil moisture data?

  • Engineered Feature: Antecedent Precipitation Index (API).
  • Result: Retraining with API resulted in worse performance (NSE dropped to -0.065). API failed to rank in the Top 5 features.
The Temporal Resolution Wall: The failure is not missing data, but daily temporal resolution. Daily averages destroy the storm intensity variance required to predict flash floods.

Final Engineering Takeaways

  • Architecture Must Match Data Variance: There is no universal "best" model. Ensembles dominate stable time-series; sequential DL captures complex perturbations slightly better.
  • Interpretability is Crucial: Classical models allowed us to extract the "Daylight" physics proxy, transforming the AI from a black box to a diagnostic tool.
  • Data Trumps Complexity: No amount of hyperparameter tuning can overcome a dataset that lacks the temporal resolution to describe the target event.
